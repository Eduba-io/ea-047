The read

BNY now operates the most visible enterprise AI platform in U.S. banking. Eliza supports over 125 live use cases, over 20,000 Empowered Builders, and a growing fleet of digital employees. The Q1 2026 slides credit AI transformation as a margin driver. The December 2025 Gemini Enterprise integration extends Eliza into agentic deep research. The next step in the roadmap, Predictive Trade Analytics that can initiate remediation autonomously, moves the platform from copilot to executor.

At that level of scale, the operating question shifts. Model quality is a solved problem inside a governed marketplace. The harder question is whether agent context is organized the same way across Securities Services, Pershing, Wealth, and Treasury Services, so that two builders writing near-identical agents produce near-identical behavior, and so that a regulator reviewing one business unit can read across to every other.

The frame we apply

Sixty, thirty, ten.

Eduba works at the orchestration layer above the model. Applied to Eliza, the useful question is not which models to pick. The useful question is which parts of an agent's context belong in structured data, which belong in approved rule sets, and which genuinely need an LLM to interpret. That separation is what makes the governance story legible to a regulator.

60%

Traditional code & data

Work that belongs in structured systems, deterministic logic, and documented data contracts. Reliable, auditable, cheap to operate at custody scale.

30%

Rule-based logic

Codified policies and approved rule sets. Where a regulator expects a written control, this is the layer that answers.

10%

Genuine AI work

The narrow slice where an LLM is the right tool. This is where interpretation and judgment earn the governance overhead.

An honest read

What the first step looks like, and what it does not.

Eduba is a six-person veteran-owned firm. We are not a panel vendor at a G-SIB, and we do not intend to be. We are a methodology partner. The first paid step is narrow and scoped to one business unit.

Narrow first scope

A two-week audit-first engagement tied to one workflow, one business unit, one owner. Methodology transfer as the deliverable.

Under TPRM

Vendor onboarding at a G-SIB takes time. We scope the first step so it can move under a lighter contract vehicle while TPRM runs its course.
